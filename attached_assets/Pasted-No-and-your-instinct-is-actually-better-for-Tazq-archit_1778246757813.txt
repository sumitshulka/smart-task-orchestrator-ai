# Why Separate Defect Management is Better

## 1. Defects are operational objects

Users often:

* search defects directly
* monitor defects separately
* assign QA teams separately
* analyze quality independently

So defects deserve their own workspace.

---

## 2. Defects may exist outside projects

Sometimes:

* production issues
* customer complaints
* platform bugs
* internal operational issues

may not belong to a formal project.

Separate module supports this.

---

## 3. QA teams think differently

Project teams think:
"What are we building?"

QA teams think:
"What is broken?"

Separate module helps both.

---

# Correct Tazq Relationship Model

Instead of:

Project
→ Defects

Use:

Defect
↔ Project (optional)
↔ Milestone (optional)
↔ Task (optional)

This is much cleaner.
