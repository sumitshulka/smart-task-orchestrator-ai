I think we overcomplicated it. Let's simplify it back to the product vision.

The Workspace is **not another module**. It is **the collaboration layer attached to every business object** in Tazq.

---

# Tazq Workspace

## Vision

A Workspace is the dedicated collaboration area for a Project, Task, Milestone or Defect where team members communicate, share files, make decisions and maintain the complete history of execution.

Unlike WhatsApp, Teams or Slack, every discussion remains permanently attached to the work item.

# The Golden Rule

I would simplify the philosophy into one sentence:

> **Every business object in Tazq should have its own intelligent workspace where conversations, files, decisions, activities and AI remain permanently associated with the work.**

---

## One enhancement I would make

Instead of calling the tabs **Discussion**, **Activity**, **Files**, **Decisions**, and **AI** separately, I would make **Workspace** a unified experience with a **single chronological timeline**.

Imagine something like Notion, Linear, or GitHub Issues, where the feed contains:

* 💬 User messages
* 📎 File uploads
* ⚙️ System activities
* ✅ Decisions
* 🤖 AI summaries

Everything appears in one place, in time order, with filters to show only messages, only files, or only activities. Users don't have to wonder which tab contains the information—they simply scroll the timeline. This design feels more modern, keeps context intact, and makes AI summarization far more effective because it has one continuous history of the work item rather than separate silos. I believe this would become one of Tazq's signature features.
